### Findings

- **One law survives the gate: LF-001** (`y = 3.67 · x^{1.40}`). Every test clears with wide margins: permutation FDR = 0.005, bootstrap CI floor = 0.999, Δbaseline = +0.50, Δconfound = +0.46 (so age + batch do **not** explain the signal), and the decoy null (shuffled `x`) fails to reproduce observed AUROC in any of 1000 trials (`decoy_p = 0.001`).
- **No overfit candidates.** Train–test AUROC gap ≤ 0.05 for every law (max gap = LF-004 at −0.031, i.e. test *above* train → no overfit concern). No law is flagged.
- **LF-002 and LF-004** fail the gate unambiguously: neither permutation nor decoy can distinguish them from chance, and the confound-only model matches or beats them (Δconfound < 0) — the `t`-axis carries no residual signal after age + batch are accounted for.
- **LF-003** is anti-predictive (test AUROC ≈ 0). Unsigned discriminatory ability is ≈ 0.9998, but the gate is directional, so it correctly falsifies the proposed `x^{−2}` form. (If inverted in sign, it would collapse onto LF-001 — treat as a rediscovery hint, not a new law.)
- **Negative control (LF-NC) fails all 5 tests**, exactly as required to validate the pipeline. Baseline confound AUROC on the flagship is 0.54, so a constant predictor cannot clear Δconfound even in principle.
