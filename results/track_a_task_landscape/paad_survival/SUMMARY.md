## Headline

**8 / 27 candidates survive** the pre-registered 5-test gate on PAAD OS survival after
pinning the falsification gene list to the 19-gene panel.

Best survivor: `sqrt((7.41/KRT17) / (CDH2 × ((1.41/CDKN2A + CD8A)/FOXP3)))`
(AUROC 0.707, Δbase +0.078).

The earlier interim `0/27` summary was generated by an unsafe inferred-column path that
included `os_months` as a numeric baseline feature. Because `label` is a median split of
`os_months`, that produced `baseline_auc=1.0` and invalid negative Δbase values. The
committed report now uses the explicit 19-gene panel and has baseline KRT17 AUROC 0.629.

## Biological note

**KRT17** remains the strongest single feature and a basal/squamous PAAD subtype marker,
but the corrected gate finds compound signal beyond KRT17 alone. The accepted family
combines basal/EMT burden (KRT17, CDH2) with immune context (FOXP3, CD8A) and
cell-cycle / cell-state terms (CDKN2A, CCNB1, CDK1, MKI67).

Interpretation: on this OS median-split task, the best PySR laws are high when basal/EMT
markers are relatively low and immune/cell-state context is high. This is not the
pre-registered GATA6/VIM lineage-vs-EMT hypothesis; it is a separate PAAD subtype/immune
context law family discovered by the unseeded search.

---

## Honest caveat

The survivor family is internally redundant and near the stability threshold
(ci_lower 0.601–0.631). Treat this as a PAAD discovery lead, not as external validation.
Independent PDAC cohorts with the same genes and survival annotations are needed before
claiming generalization.
